Drunk Driving Deaths in the United States are Steadily Declining

By MAUREEN OSEI | FEB 25 2015*

Every year, millions of automobile collisions occur in the United States alone. Consequentially, hundreds of thousands of people lose their lives -- those behind the wheel and passengers alike. Additionally, many of these car accidents and deaths occur due to driving under the influence. Among these fatalities, more than half were passengers of vehicles with drivers who had BACs greater than or equal to 0.08%.

*Rate of deaths per 100,000 population for people involved in crashes involving a driver with a BAC greater than or equal to 0.08%

Fortunately, since 2012, alcohol-impaired driving mortality rates have been on a steady decline.

The decline of drunk driving mortality rates can be attributed to several factors including strengthening impaired driving laws and enforcement efforts, education and awareness campaigns, and the use of technology (e.g., ignition interlocks) to prevent impaired drivers from operating vehicles.

Although all states have laws against driving while impaired, some states have additional measures to further discourage impaired driving. These measures include sobriety checkpoints and campaigns such as “Drive Sober or Get Pulled Over”. The states that implement these addtional measures, such as Maryland and Ohio, have experienced a larger drop in mortality rates than those that fail to do so.

*Rate of deaths per 100,000 population for people involved in crashes involving a driver with a BAC greater than or equal to 0.08%
